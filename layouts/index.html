What inaction looks like

The cost is rarely visible until it is expensive.

The gap does not announce itself. It shows up as the pilot that passes every technical review and still never reaches production, because no one owns the decision to deploy it. As the governance model that satisfies the auditor while no one in the building can actually interpret what it flags. As the third initiative to stall at the same point as the first two, each time explained away as a delivery problem.

None of these read as structural on a status report, which is precisely why they persist. The technology keeps working. The operating model keeps refusing it. The cost compounds quietly, in time, in credibility, and in the options you no longer have by the time the pattern becomes undeniable.
